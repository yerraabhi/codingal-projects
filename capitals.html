1 Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh.

In English, Lucknow (/ˈlʌknaʊ/) is the name of a major city in northern India, specifically the capital and largest city of the state of Uttar Pradesh. It serves as an important center for education, commerce, culture, and industry, and it was the site of a significant British residency during the Indian Mutiny (1857).


2 Jaipur, Rajasthan.

Jaipur is the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Rajasthan, located in northwest India. Also known as the "Pink City," it is famous for its picturesque pink sandstone buildings and was founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, making it one of India's first planned cities. Jaipur is a major tourist attraction, known for its historical forts, palaces, and monuments, and is a gateway to other desert cities in the state.


3 Mumbai, Maharashtra.

Mumbai is the capital city of the state of Maharashtra in western India, located on the Konkan coast of the Arabian Sea. It is a major port and commercial, industrial, and financial center in India, as well as the center of the Indian film industry. Mumbai is India's largest city by population and was officially known as Bombay until 1995.


New Delhi

In English, New Delhi (proper noun) refers to the capital city of India and a part of the national capital territory of Delhi. It is located in the northern part of India, just south of the old city of Delhi. Figuratively, "New Delhi" can also refer to the government of India.


Hyderabad

In English, "Hyderabad" refers to either a major city in south-central India, the capital of Telangana state, known for its history and monuments, or a city in southwest Pakistan on the Indus River. The name itself translates to "Lion City," originating from the Arabic word for lion, Haydar, and the suffix "-abad" meaning settlement.
