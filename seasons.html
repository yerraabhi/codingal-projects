Seasons of India

India has 4 seasons and we will learn about all of them.

  1. Winter

    2. The winter season in India generally spans from December to February, with January often being the coldest month. While the northern regions experience colder temperatures, including potential snowfall in the Himalayas, the southern parts of India generally enjoy milder winters.

  2. Summer

    3. In India, the summer season typically spans from March to June, with the hottest months being May and June. This period is characterized by high temperatures, low humidity, and the potential for heat waves, especially in the northern and central regions. The sun's rays are nearly vertical during this time, causing the midday heat to be intense.

  3. Monsoon

    4. The rainy season in India, also known as the monsoon season, typically lasts from June to September. During this period, India receives the majority of its annual rainfall, with the southwest monsoon bringing moisture-laden winds from the ocean to the land.

  4. Autumn

    5. Autumn in India, also known as Sharad Ritu, generally falls between October and November. This season follows the monsoon and precedes winter, characterized by pleasant, cooler temperatures, clear skies, and vibrant foliage as trees begin to shed their leaves. The monsoon rains have subsided, leaving behind fresh, crisp air.
